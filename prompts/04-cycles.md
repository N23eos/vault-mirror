# 04 — Cycles: Started, Abandoned, Finished

## Goal

Map the mechanics of how this person starts, drops, and (sometimes) finishes
things — projects, habits, learning efforts, ideas. Find the repeating cycle,
not just the inventory.

## Method

- Build an inventory of undertakings found in the vault: projects, courses,
  habit attempts, big ideas. For each: first mention, last mention, apparent
  outcome (finished / abandoned / faded / ongoing / unknown).
- For abandoned items, look for the drop point: what was the last note about
  it, what was happening around that time, was there a stated reason or just
  silence?
- For finished items, look for what was different: external deadline, partner,
  smaller scope, higher stakes?
- Extract the typical lifecycle: how long from spark to peak to drop? Is there
  a recurring phase where things die?
- Count honestly. Rough ratios (started : finished) matter more than
  precision.

## Output contract — `Analysis/04-cycles.md`

```
# Cycles
## Inventory           (table: undertaking → span → outcome → evidence link)
## The typical cycle   (phases with typical durations)
## Where things die    (the recurring drop point, with examples)
## What survives       (common traits of finished things)
## Ratios              (started vs finished, by period if visible)
```

## Rules reminder

Apply the global rules from `prompts/00-start.md`.

## Quality bar

- Does the inventory include at least the major undertakings across the whole
  timeline, not just recent ones?
- Is "where things die" a mechanism, not a moral judgment?
