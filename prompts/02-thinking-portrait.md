# 02 — Thinking Portrait

## Goal

Describe how this person's mind actually works — not what they think, but how:
structures, habits, strengths and quirks of their thinking as evidenced by the
notes themselves.

## Method

- Sample across periods and content types (use the methodology from
  `Analysis/01-corpus-survey.md`).
- Look at form, not just content: how notes are structured, how ideas start
  and end, list vs prose, questions vs assertions, how often notes link to or
  build on each other.
- Signals to hunt: recurring reasoning moves (analogy, first principles,
  enumeration), abstraction level, how disagreement with self is handled,
  whether ideas get revisited or abandoned, planning style, degree of
  self-reflection vs pure task capture.
- Compare early notes to recent ones: has the thinking style changed?

## Output contract — `Analysis/02-thinking-portrait.md`

```
# Thinking Portrait
## Core style          (3–5 traits, each with evidence)
## Reasoning moves     (recurring patterns with examples)
## Structure habits    (how notes are built; what that implies)
## Evolution           (then vs now)
## Blind side of the style  (what this way of thinking systematically misses)
```

## Rules reminder

Apply the global rules from `prompts/00-start.md`.

## Quality bar

- Every trait has at least one quote or `[[wikilink]]`.
- Would the portrait distinguish this person from a generic "curious
  note-taker"? If it fits anyone, sharpen it.
