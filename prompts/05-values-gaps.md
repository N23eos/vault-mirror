# 05 — Values vs Behavior

## Goal

Compare what the person says they value with what their notes show they
actually spend attention on — and map the gaps without moralizing.

## Method

- Collect declared values: explicit statements ("what matters to me"), goals
  lists, new-year notes, principles, admired qualities in others.
- Collect revealed values: where the writing time actually goes, what gets
  detailed and careful treatment vs a throwaway line, what they return to
  unprompted, what they defend when arguing with themselves.
- Match the two lists. Three buckets: aligned (declared and lived),
  gap (declared but little behavioral trace), shadow (lived but never
  declared — often the most interesting bucket).
- For each gap, look for the cost being paid and any signs the person already
  senses it.

## Output contract — `Analysis/05-values-gaps.md`

```
# Values vs Behavior
## Declared values     (with sources)
## Revealed values     (with the behavioral evidence)
## Aligned             (where words and notes agree)
## Gaps                (declared but not lived — evidence for both sides)
## Shadow values       (lived but not declared)
## What the gaps cost  (marked with confidence levels)
```

## Rules reminder

Apply the global rules from `prompts/00-start.md`.

## Quality bar

- Is every gap supported by evidence on BOTH sides (the declaration and the
  absence)?
- Zero moralizing — gaps are described, not judged.
