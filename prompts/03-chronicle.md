# 03 — Chronicle

## Goal

Reconstruct the person's story as told by the vault: periods, turning points,
what dominated attention when, and how focus migrated over time.

## Method

- Use the timeline from `Analysis/01-corpus-survey.md` as the skeleton.
- For each year (or denser periods if the vault is young), sample notes and
  identify: dominant themes, active projects, emotional tone, notable events
  mentioned.
- Mark turning points: theme appears/dies, tone shifts, writing volume spikes
  or collapses. A silence in the vault is data too — flag gaps and offer
  hypotheses for them.
- Distinguish what happened (events referenced) from what occupied the mind
  (what got written about). These often diverge — that divergence is signal.

## Output contract — `Analysis/03-chronicle.md`

```
# Chronicle
## Period map          (table: period → dominant themes → tone → volume)
## Period narratives   (one short section per period, evidence-linked)
## Turning points      (moments where the vault visibly changes)
## Silences            (gaps and volume collapses, with hypotheses)
## Through-lines       (themes that persist across all periods)
```

## Rules reminder

Apply the global rules from `prompts/00-start.md`.

## Quality bar

- Is every turning point anchored to specific notes/dates?
- Are hypotheses about silences marked `hypothesis`, not stated as fact?
